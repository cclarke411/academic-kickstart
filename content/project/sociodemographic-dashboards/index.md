This project area captures my work on socio-demographic dashboards and related internal decision-support tooling. In the OpenAI export, it appears through titles such as **Interactive Dashboards**, **Interactive Staff Dashboard**, **A Scholarly Knowledge Graph-Powered Dashboard**, and **Socio-Demographic Data Dashboards Technical Appendix**.

Together, those titles point to a broader pattern of work: not just designing visual outputs, but also documenting how those systems should function and how they should be interpreted. The inclusion of a technical appendix is especially important because it suggests attention to methodology, indicator logic, system assumptions, or implementation details rather than presentation alone.

The staff-dashboard framing indicates a focus on operational usefulness. Dashboards for internal users have different requirements than public-facing visualizations: they need to help teams monitor activity, spot patterns, compare groups, and make better day-to-day decisions. That usually means the work involves audience definition, metric selection, information hierarchy, and careful translation from data structure to human workflow.

The knowledge-graph thread adds another dimension. It suggests that I was exploring ways to move beyond flat descriptive reporting toward richer relationship-aware analytics, where entities, concepts, and interactions can be represented more meaningfully. Even where that work remains conceptual or exploratory, it shows a willingness to think about data products as reasoning environments rather than just static charts.

This project represents my interest in building analytic tools that are interpretable, operationally useful, and supported by enough technical documentation to make them sustainable. It reflects a combination of dashboard design, technical communication, and systems thinking applied to socio-demographic data.
