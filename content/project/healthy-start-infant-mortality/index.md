This body of work centered on public-health analysis and communication related to **Healthy Start** and **infant mortality**. The OpenAI export manifest includes a Healthy Start proposal, a Healthy Start data report, and multiple visual assets covering California infant mortality, Maryland infant mortality, and top counties by infant mortality rate.

The project appears to have involved more than producing charts in isolation. The combination of proposal writing, report drafting, and comparative visuals suggests I was building a decision-support narrative that could help stakeholders understand both the data and the implications behind it. In practice, that means organizing statistical material into a form that can support planning, funding discussions, and program strategy.

I worked across several levels of analysis. State-level and county-level views imply attention to geographic variation, while the report and proposal materials indicate that the analysis had to be converted into coherent written arguments as well. That combination matters because many health-equity projects fail when the numbers and the narrative are disconnected. My role here was to help bridge that gap by making the evidence readable, interpretable, and useful.

This kind of project also requires sensitivity to audience. A technical analyst may want methodological detail, while a program stakeholder may need a clear summary of where the burden is concentrated, what patterns stand out, and why intervention priorities should be adjusted. The materials in the archive suggest I was doing that translation work by aligning data visuals with proposal and reporting language.

Taken together, this project represents applied data analysis in a mission-driven context: using quantitative evidence and visual communication to support understanding of maternal and infant health disparities, strengthen reporting, and contribute to public-health planning.
